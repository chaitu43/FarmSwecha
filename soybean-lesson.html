Introduction to Soybean

Soybean is often called the 'Miracle Crop' due to its high nutritional value, containing approx 40% protein and 20% oil. In Andhra Pradesh, it is gaining popularity as a lucrative pulse-cum-oilseed crop, especially in areas with well-managed irrigation.

Nutrient Efficiency

Soybeans are efficient nitrogen fixers. When properly inoculated with the right strain of Bradyrhizobium japonicum, they can fulfill most of their own nitrogen needs.

Step 1: Soil & Seed Bed

Soybeans prefer well-drained, fertile silty-clay loams; a fine seedbed is critical for its large seeds.

Detailed Process:

  1. Field Preparation: Plough the field 2-3 times to ensure it is free from clods and debris.
  2. Variety Selection: Choose high-yielding varieties with good resistance to pod shattering (e.g., JS 335, Bragg).
  3. Seed Inoculation: Mix seeds with Bradyrhizobium japonicum culture to ensure healthy nitrogen fixation.
  4. Sowing Configuration: Maintain 45 cm between rows and 5-10 cm between plants for optimal light capture.
  5. Depth Control: Plant seeds at a shallow depth of 3-4 cm; avoid deeper sowing to ensure uniform emergence.

Step 2: Node Management

The number of nodes on the stem directly correlates with the potential pod count; balanced nutrition is essential.

Detailed Process:

  1. Basal Nutrition: Apply 20 kg N and 60-80 kg P2O5 per hectare basally to support early node development.
  2. Potassium Support: Ensure adequate Potassium (20-40 kg/ha) for stem strength and disease resistance.
  3. Avoiding Over-Nitrogen: Do not over-apply nitrogen fertilizers, as this can lead to weak stems and 'lodging' (falling over).
  4. Micronutrient Check: If growth is stunted, consider a Zinc or Sulfur supplement based on soil requirements.

Step 3: Critical Irrigation

Moisture stress during reproductive phases is the leading cause of yield loss in soybean.

Detailed Process:

  1. Flower Monitoring: Ensure soil remains moist when the first flowers appear on the nodes.
  2. Pod Filling Phase: Maintain moisture during the beam-filling/pod-filling stage to avoid shrivelled seeds.
  3. Irrigation Logic: provide 2-3 supplemental irrigations if the monsoon rains fail during these windows.
  4. Waterlogging Caution: Ensure ridges or drains are prepared to prevent standing water during heavy spells.

Step 4: Harvest Timing

Harvesting at the right moisture level maintains grain quality and prevents storage losses.

Detailed Process:

  1. Maturity Identification: Harvest when 95% of pods turn grey/brown and leaves have fallen from the plant.
  2. Physical Check: Seeds should produce a rattling sound when the pods are shaken.
  3. Harvest Method: Cut the plants low or use a harvester once the moisture is around 14-15%.
  4. Threshing: Use a mechanical thresher at a low cylinder speed to prevent delicate seed cracking.
  5. Cleaning & Drying: Clean the grains of all debris and dry until moisture reaches ~10%.
  6. Ventilated Storage: Store in a cool, well-ventilated area to prevent oil rancidity and maintain seed vigor.

Pro Tip: Critical Irrigation

The most critical stages for moisture in Soybean are the flowering and pod-filling stages. A moisture stress during these times can lead to aborted flowers and shriveled seeds, drastically reducing yield.
