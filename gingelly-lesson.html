The Complete Guide to Gingelly (Sesame)

Introduction to Gingelly

Sesame, known as Nuvvulu in Andhra Pradesh, is one of the oldest oilseed crops. It is highly valued for its oil quality and medicinal properties. It is a short-duration crop (80-100 days) and consumes very little water, making it a favorite for dry seasons.

Water Efficiency

Sesame requires minimal irrigation after the initial establishment phase. It is highly sensitive to waterlogging; even a few hours of standing water can rot the roots.

Step 1: Sowing & Spacing

Sesame seeds are extremely small; precision sowing is vital for a uniform stand.

Detailed Process:

  1. Seed Mixing: Blend the tiny seeds with dry sand or well-decomposed FYM in a 1:4 ratio to ensure even distribution.
  2. Variety Pick: Choose varieties like G-8 or Hima for high oil content and disease tolerance.
  3. Precision Depth: Sow the seeds at a shallow depth of only 1-2 cm; any deeper will hinder emergence.
  4. Optimal Spacing: maintain 30 cm between rows and 15-20 cm between plants after thinning.
  5. Thinning Timing: Perform thinning 15-20 days after sowing to maintain the desired plant density.

Step 2: Phyllody Management

Phyllody is a major viral-like disease that can cause 100% loss by turning flowers into leaf-like structures.

Detailed Process:

  1. Early Detection: Inspect the crop regularly from the 30th day onwards for any plants with malformed, green flowers.
  2. Immediate Roguing: Pull out and bury infected plants as soon as symptoms appear to stop the spread.
  3. Vector Monitoring: Monitor for leafhoppers (Jassids), the primary carriers of the disease, using yellow sticky traps.
  4. Integrated Defense: Choose tolerant varieties and avoid continuous sesame cultivation in the same field to break the cycle.

Step 3: Watering Needs

Sesame is highly water-efficient but remains extremely sensitive to moisture extremes.

Detailed Process:

  1. Establishment Phase: ensure the soil has enough moisture during the germination and early seedling stages.
  2. Critical Flowering Period: Provide a light irrigation during the flowering and capsule formation stages if no rain occurs.
  3. Waterlogging Prevention: ensure perfect drainage; even a few hours of standing water can cause sudden wilting and death.
  4. Rabi/Summer Scheduling: In non-rainy seasons, schedule 3-4 light irrigations at 10-15 day intervals depending on soil type.

Step 4: Selective Harvesting

Sesame capsules mature unevenly; harvesting at the right stage prevents seed shattering.

Detailed Process:

  1. Maturity Clues: Harvest when the bottom-most capsules start turning yellow and the leaves begin to drop.
  2. Shocking Technique: Cut the plants at the base and stack them vertically in 'shocks' to dry and allow upper pods to mature.
  3. Sun Curing: let the plants dry for 3-5 days in the sun until all capsules turn brown and begin to split.
  4. Threshing: Hold the dried bundles upside down over a clean cloth and gently beat them to release the seeds.
  5. Final Drying: Dry the cleaned seeds on a floor for 2-3 days until the moisture content is 5% for long-term safety.

Pro Tip: Phyllody Disease

Phyllody is a serious disease where floral parts turn into green leaf-like structures, resulting in no seed setting. Remove and destroy infected plants immediately and control the leafhoppers that spread the disease.
